What we know about COVID-19?

Covid-19 is the disease caused by a new Coronavirus (SARS-Cov-2) that first appeared in Wuhan, China in late 2019, probably at a wild live animal market.

SARS-Cov-2

SARS-CoV-2 is a new type of virus that belongs to the large family of Coronaviruses. Coronaviruses are responsible for causing diseases in animals and humans, like, diarrhea, nose and throat infections. SARS and MERS were other diseases caused by Coronaviruses.

COVID-19

It's a pretty deadly virus. It spreads very fast. People with no symptoms can spread the virus. You can get it by breathing the air infected with the virus by sneezing or coughing, and by touching infected things and then touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with your dirty hand.

Who can get infected?

Everyone! Kids and teenagers can get infected as everyone but do not get seriously ill.
Elderlies and people with other illnesses or diseases can die from COVID-19 because of breathing difficulties or failures in organs.
